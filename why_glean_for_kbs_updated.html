The case for acting now

KBS doesn’t need “another tool” — it needs a governed Work AI layer

Status quo, embedded AI in individual apps, or bespoke builds on raw LLMs all share the same flaw: they don’t solve the cross-system, cross-role knowledge problem. KBS needs one context layer that understands its people, customers, contracts, sites, and systems — and that can power search, assistant, and agents with the same governed foundation.

Why change?
High-friction work in high-leverage roles
Strategic Account Managers, Finance, Legal, HR, IT, and Corporate Development spend disproportionate time:
  • Rebuilding margin models for every rebid, renewal, and award template from scratch.
  • Manually stitching together customer, pricing, and operations data from Microsoft 365, NetSuite, Salesforce, and KBS Force.
  • Re‑explaining the same decisions to new leaders and deal teams instead of executing the next opportunity.
Why Glean?
Margin, revenue, and quality outcomes
Glean is the Work AI platform that connects KBS’s tech stack to its business outcomes:
  • Protect margin by reducing time spent on low‑value searching and rework, and by giving Pricing and Finance fast access to prior deals, economics, and risks.
  • Grow revenue by helping SAMs and Sales quickly assemble QBRs, renewals, and award packages that surface the full KBS story and capacity for more business.
  • Ensure quality service by giving Operations, IT, and HR instant access to playbooks, standards, and incident history that keep performance consistent across every site.
Why now?
Window to set the Work AI standard
Approving Glean now lets KBS:
  • Standardize on a governed AI platform before tools and shadow IT proliferate.
  • Launch a scoped Year 1 pilot to prove ROI on a subset of roles and workflows before expanding.
  • Build AI fluency and trust with the same discipline KBS applies to safety, compliance, and operations.

Where the hours come from

Targeted workflows across customer teams, engineering, and support

Glean’s value at KBS is concentrated in a small number of high-friction, repeatable workflows. These are not “nice to have” use cases — they sit at the heart of revenue, margin, and customer experience.

Cross‑company foundation
  • Unified enterprise search – One permission-aware search across Microsoft 365, Teams, SharePoint, NetSuite, Salesforce, structured databases, and KBS Force, so employees spend less time hunting and more time executing.
  • Work assistant for every knowledge worker – Drafts QBR slides, summarizes threads, explains contracts, generates recaps, and answers “have we done this before?” from KBS’s own history.
  • Onboarding accelerators – New managers and analysts ramp faster by querying past deals, playbooks, and policies instead of rediscovering them.
Customer & commercial workflows
  • Award template & pricing agents – Turn multi-hour spreadsheet gymnastics into a guided, minutes‑long flow that pulls the right inputs from NetSuite, Salesforce, KBS Force, and prior awards.
  • QBR / renewal prep agent – Automatically compiles performance, incidents, scope changes, pricing history, and prior commitments into a review-ready outline for SAMs.
  • KYC / customer 360 – One question yields the latest contracts, SLAs, scope of work, change orders, pricing terms, and open risk items for any major account.
Engineering & KBS Force
  • Project onboarding & context – Engineers ask natural-language questions about a system or integration and get history across tickets, runbooks, and documentation.
  • On‑call & incident triage – For any incident, Glean surfaces similar past issues, fixes, and post‑mortems across ticketing, logs, and internal docs.
  • PR review copilot – Summarizes changes, highlights risky areas, and links to prior related work to speed reviews and reduce regressions.
IT / HR / support
  • Ticket deflection & faster MTTR – An assistant that can answer common IT/HR questions and help triage complex ones using policy docs, SOPs, and prior tickets, driving fewer tickets and lower mean time to resolution.
  • Policy & benefits assistant – Employees self‑serve answers on leave, travel, pay, and benefits, reducing repetitive HR inquiries.
  • Onboarding & M&A playbooks – Agents that assemble onboarding plans and diligence packs from prior deals and playbooks, lowering the labor cost of each transaction.
Path to value

Start with search & assistant, then layer in agents, pilot, and expansion

The deployment plan is intentionally gated. Phase 2 and Phase 3 only unlock once Phase 1 (and any scoped pilot) has demonstrated adoption, time savings, and clear executive confidence in the model.

Phase 1 — Unified search & assistant ~30–60 days

Connect core systems (Microsoft 365, Teams, SharePoint, NetSuite, Salesforce, structured databases, and KBS Force). Roll out to ~1,000 targeted users (or a smaller pilot cohort) with clear comms, training, and baselines. Measure search and assistant adoption, time savings, and sentiment across key roles.

Phase 2 — High‑value agents Months 3–6

Codify the highest‑impact workflows into agents: award templates, QBR prep, KYC / customer 360, engineering onboarding and triage, and ticket deflection. Validate modeled savings against real behavior and refine the ROI model with KBS Finance based on pilot results.

Phase 3 — Expand & embed Months 6–12

Expand seat coverage and agent exposure to additional locations and functions, and embed Glean into where KBS already works (Teams, ticketing, CRM, and internal portals). Use the proven ROI, capacity gains, and service-quality improvements to inform staffing, budgeting, and future AI investments.

Recommendation

Approve Glean as the Work AI platform for KBS and proceed with a scoped Year 1 pilot leading to a 1,000‑user deployment

The ROI model, deployment plan, and use case portfolio show a clear, operationally credible path to value tied directly to margin, revenue growth, and quality service. Glean gives KBS a governed, cross‑system AI foundation at the exact moment when margin pressure, customer expectations, and AI experimentation converge.
