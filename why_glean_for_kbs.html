The case for acting now

KBS doesn’t need “another tool” — it needs a governed Work AI layer

Status quo, embedded AI in individual apps, or bespoke builds on raw LLMs all share the same flaw: they don’t solve the cross-system knowledge problem. KBS needs one context layer that understands its people, customers, contracts, sites, and systems — and that can power search, assistant, and agents with the same governed foundation.

Why change?
High-friction work in high-leverage roles
Strategic Account Managers, Finance, Legal, HR, IT, and Corporate Development spend disproportionate time:
  • Rebuilding context for every rebid, renewal, and award template.
  • Manually synthesizing data across email, Excel, NetSuite, Salesforce, SharePoint, and KBS Force.
  • Re-answering the same questions for new leaders, new deals, and new acquisitions.
Why Glean?
One Work AI layer across KBS
Glean is a single-tenant, permission-aware Work AI platform that:
  • Connects M365, SharePoint, Teams, Salesforce, NetSuite, Fresh, Workday, and KBS Force with document-level security.
  • Serves roles, not apps: SAMs, Finance, Legal, HR, IT, Engineering.
  • Co-exists with Copilot and Bedrock as the front door and orchestration layer, not a competing point solution.
Why now?
Window to set the Work AI standard
Approving Glean now lets KBS:
  • Standardize on a governed AI platform before tools and shadow IT proliferate.
  • Prove value in measurable workflows (awards, QBRs, support, onboarding) in 2026.
  • Build AI fluency and trust across KBS with clear governance and observability.

Where the hours come from

Targeted workflows across customer teams, engineering, and support

Glean’s value at KBS is concentrated in a small number of high-friction, repeatable workflows. These are not “nice to have” use cases — they sit at the heart of revenue, margin, and customer experience.

Cross‑company foundation
  • Unified enterprise search – One permission-aware search across M365, SharePoint, Teams, Salesforce, NetSuite, Fresh, Workday, and KBS Force, so employees spend less time hunting and more time executing.
  • Work assistant for every knowledge worker – Drafts QBR slides, summarizes threads, explains contracts, generates recaps, and answers “have we done this before?” from KBS’s own history.
  • Onboarding accelerators – New managers and analysts ramp faster by querying past deals, playbooks, and policies instead of rediscovering them.
Customer & commercial workflows
  • Award template & pricing agents – Turn multi-hour spreadsheet gymnastics into a guided, minutes-long flow that pulls the right inputs from NetSuite, Salesforce, KBS Force, and prior awards.
  • QBR / renewal prep agent – Automatically compiles performance, incidents, scope changes, pricing history, and prior commitments into a review-ready outline for SAMs.
  • KYC / customer 360 – One question yields the latest contracts, SLAs, scope of work, change orders, pricing terms, and open risk items for any major account.
Engineering & KBS Force
  • Project onboarding & context – Engineers ask natural-language questions about a system or integration and get history across tickets, runbooks, and documentation.
  • On‑call & incident triage – For any incident, Glean surfaces similar past issues, fixes, and post‑mortems across Fresh, logs, and internal docs.
  • PR review copilot – Summarizes changes, highlights risky areas, and links to prior related work to speed reviews and reduce regressions.
IT / HR / support
  • Ticket deflection & faster MTTR – An assistant that can answer common IT/HR questions and help triage complex ones using policy docs, SOPs, and prior tickets, driving fewer tickets and lower mean time to resolution.
  • Policy & benefits assistant – Employees self‑serve answers on leave, travel, pay, and benefits, reducing repetitive HR inquiries.
  • Onboarding & M&A playbooks – Agents that assemble onboarding plans and diligence packs from prior deals and playbooks, lowering the labor cost of each transaction.
Path to value

Start with search & assistant, then layer in agents & expansion

The deployment plan is intentionally gated. Phase 2 and Phase 3 only unlock once Phase 1 has demonstrated adoption, time savings, and clear executive confidence in the model.

Phase 1 — Unified search & assistant ~30–60 days

Connect core systems (M365, SharePoint, Teams, Salesforce, NetSuite, Fresh, Workday pilot data, KBS Force). Roll out to ~1,000 targeted users with clear comms, training, and baselines. Measure search and assistant adoption, time savings, and sentiment across key roles.

Phase 2 — High‑value agents Months 3–6

Codify the highest‑impact workflows into agents: award templates, QBR prep, KYC / customer 360, engineering onboarding and triage, and ticket deflection. Validate modeled savings against real behavior and refine the ROI model with KBS Finance.

Phase 3 — Expand & embed Months 6–12

Expand seat coverage and agent exposure to additional locations and functions, and embed Glean into where KBS already works (Teams, ticketing, CRM, and internal portals). Use the proven ROI and capacity gains to inform staffing, budgeting, and future AI investments.

Recommendation

Approve Glean as the Work AI platform for KBS and proceed with the initial 1,000‑user deployment

The ROI model, deployment plan, and use case portfolio show a clear, operationally credible path to value. Glean gives KBS a governed, cross‑system AI foundation at the exact moment when margin pressure, customer expectations, and AI experimentation converge.
